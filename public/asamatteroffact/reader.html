INTEGRAL TRANSFORMS
INTEGRALS CAN SEPERATE FUNCTIONS
THE KERNEL

What if you have an equation that doesn't fit any of our guessing strategies?

$y''(t)+y(t)=\sin(t)$

How would you solve an equation like this? What if you could move this equation through a portal that reqrites it in a different coordinate system where the math is just easier?

This is the idea of an Integral Transform. Integrals are LINEAR OPERATORS meaning that you can split an integral into components of integrals added together. That is $\int(5+2)dx = \int(5)dx+\int(2)dx$. Integrals are also invertible meaning that all information on the function is retained upon integration ad you can retrieve the original function by simply differentiating back. Basically, by integrating, we do not loose the properties of the original function, so we can use this to change our function into an equivalent form where the math just so happens to be easier, then simply change our solution back and our problem is solved!
